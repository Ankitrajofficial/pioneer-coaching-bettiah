How to plan your Class 12 board revision

Home / Blog / Article
Exam Strategy · 5 min read

The last two months before boards decide a lot. Most students don't lack knowledge — they lack a plan. Here is a simple structure we use with our Class 12 students.

1. Finish the syllabus 6 weeks early

Aim to complete every chapter at least six weeks before the exam. The final weeks are for revision and practice, not first-time learning.

2. Rotate subjects daily

Do not study only one subject for days. Touch both Maths and Biology every day so nothing goes cold.

  • Morning: the subject you find harder (full focus)
  • Evening: the subject you enjoy (lighter revision)

3. Solve previous-year papers under timer

From a month out, solve at least two full papers a week with a strict clock. This builds speed and exposes weak spots while there is still time to fix them.

4. Maintain an error notebook

Every mistake goes into one notebook. In the final week you revise only this notebook — it is the highest-value revision you can do.

Stick to the plan, trust the process, and the marks follow.
