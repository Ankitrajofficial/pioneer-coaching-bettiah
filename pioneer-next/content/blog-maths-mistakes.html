5 common mistakes students make in Maths

Home / Blog / Article
Mathematics · 4 min read

In Maths, you rarely lose marks because a problem is too hard. You lose them to small, avoidable habits. Fix these five and watch your score climb.

1. Skipping steps

Writing every step earns method marks even when the final answer slips. Never solve big chunks in your head.

2. Not practising enough

Maths is a skill, not a topic to read. Solve daily — consistency beats long, rare study sessions.

3. Ignoring the NCERT examples

Solved examples teach the exact method expected in exams. Work through them before the exercises.

4. Poor time management

  • Attempt the questions you know first
  • Do not get stuck on one tough sum
  • Leave time to recheck calculations

5. Not reviewing mistakes

Keep an error notebook and revisit it weekly. The mistakes you fix are the marks you gain.

Small corrections, big results — that is the Maths game.
